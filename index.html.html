How to Use This Tracker

Three trackers. One week. Complete picture of your evenings.

🌙

Sleep Resistance Tracker

Track how long bedtime takes, your child's resistance level and what helped — or didn't — each night.

😤

Tantrum Tracker

Log tantrum triggers, time of day, what caused the meltdown and what helped your child regulate.

💧

Dry Night Tracker

Track evening fluids, toilet trips and dry or wet night results to spot the patterns causing night wakings.

Four Simple Steps

1
Print all pages and keep them together on your fridge or bedside table.
2
Fill in each tracker every evening and morning for 7 full days.
3
Be consistent. Even imperfect data reveals more than no data at all.
4
Review page 5 at the end of the week and complete the reflection questions.

💛 A Note for You

You are not tracking to judge yourself or your child. You are tracking to understand. One week of data will show you more than months of guessing ever could. Be patient. Be curious. And be kind to yourself throughout.

Start Earlier

Begin the bedtime routine before the cortisol spike hits. Most children need to start winding down 60 to 90 minutes before target sleep.

📵

Screen Cut-off

End screen time 90 minutes before bed. This alone reduces resistance significantly within the first week for most families.

🎙️

Lower Your Voice

At the first sign of resistance, lower and slow your voice. Your calm is contagious — so is your stress. Choose deliberately.

Label the Feeling

Instead of stop crying — say It seems like you are really frustrated right now. Name the feeling 3 to 4 times before solving anything.

🔽

Get Low

Kneel down to your child's eye level. Physical height signals authority and can escalate. Eye level signals safety and connection.

⏳

Wait for the Shift

After labelling 3 to 4 times, wait. You will see the breath change and body soften. Only then introduce a boundary or solution.

Fluid Cut-off

Reduce fluids 1 to 2 hours before bed. Water only if needed — no juice or milk in the evening.

🚽

Double Voiding

Have your child use the toilet twice before sleep — once at the start of the routine and once right before lights out.

💛

No Shame Ever

Respond to wet nights with warmth every time. Your calm response is more powerful than any strategy.
